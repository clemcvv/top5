TOP V Formula 1 crash of the 21st century

TOP 5 Robert Kubica - 2007 Canadian Grand Prix

During the Canadian Grand Prix in 2007, Robert Kubica suffered a huge accident. His BMW Sauber hit the wall at over 230 km/h, went airborne and ended up in the opposite barrier. Fortunately, he escaped with only a sprained ankle and concussion.

TOP 4 Mark Webber - European Grand Prix 2010

During the European Grand Prix at Valencia in 2010, Mark Webber crashed into the rear of Heikki Kovalainen's Lotus, sending his Red Bull flying through the air before crashing heavily into the ground. Fortunately, Webber escaped unhurt.

TOP 3 Fernando Alonso - Australian Grand Prix 2016

Alonso suffered a spectacular accident at the 2016 Australian Grand Prix. His McLaren collided with the rear of Esteban Gutiérrez's Haas, causing the car to barrel before crashing into the barriers. Miraculously, Alonso emerged from the car without serious injury.

TOP 2 Romain Grosjean - Bahrain Grand Prix 2020

Grosjean was involved in one of the most terrifying accidents in recent F1 history. After making contact with Daniil Kvyat's car, his Haas slammed into the barriers at 220 km/h and burst into flames. Thanks to his composure and the efficiency of the emergency services, he escaped with burns to his hands.

TOP 1 Felipe Massa - Hungarian Grand Prix 2009

During qualifying for the 2009 Hungarian Grand Prix, a loose part from Rubens Barrichello's car hit Felipe Massa's helmet at full speed, leaving him unconscious. He hit the wall at high speed, but fortunately recovered after a long convalescence.
